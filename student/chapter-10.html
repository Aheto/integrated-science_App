üìò Plant Nutrients: Feeding Our Crops

Plants need nutrients to grow strong‚Äîjust like humans need food. These nutrients come from two main types of sources: organic (from living or once-living matter) and inorganic (chemically made).

üå± Organic Nutrient Sources

These come from natural, biodegradable materials:

  • Compost: Decayed kitchen scraps and plant waste
  • Animal manure: Cow dung, poultry droppings
  • Green manure: Planted crops like beans turned into soil
  • Wood ash: From cooking fires (adds potassium)

‚úÖ Benefits: Improves soil structure, holds water, and releases nutrients slowly.

üß™ Inorganic Nutrient Sources

These are factory-made chemical fertilizers:

  • NPK fertilizer: Contains Nitrogen (N), Phosphorus (P), Potassium (K)
  • Urea: High in nitrogen
  • Ammonium nitrate: Fast-acting nitrogen source

‚ö†Ô∏è Caution: Overuse can harm soil microbes and pollute rivers.

üí≠ Thought Experiment: If a farmer uses only inorganic fertilizer for 10 years, what might happen to the soil? How could compost help?
