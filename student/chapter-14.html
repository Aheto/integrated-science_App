üìò How Animals Support Life and Livelihoods

Animals are not just pets‚Äîthey are vital to survival and income in Ghanaian homes and markets. Their uses fall into two categories: domestic (for home use) and commercial (for sale and profit).

üè° Domestic Uses (For the Home)

  • Food: Eggs (chickens), milk (cattle), meat (goats, sheep)
  • Manure: Fertilizer for crops (from cattle, poultry, goats)
  • Security: Dogs guard homes and farms
  • Companionship: Cats and dogs as pets
  • Work: Donkeys carry loads; oxen plough fields

üí∞ Commercial Uses (For Income)

  • Meat sales: Goat, sheep, chicken, and pork at markets
  • Egg production: Layer chickens supply eggs to shops
  • Milk and dairy: Sold fresh or processed (yogurt, cheese)
  • Skin and hides: Used for leather goods (bags, shoes)
  • Live animal trade: Sold during festivals (Eid, Christmas)
üí≠ Thought Experiment: A family raises 20 chickens. They eat 5 eggs per day and sell the rest. If each chicken lays 1 egg/day, how many eggs can they sell daily? How does this support their income?
