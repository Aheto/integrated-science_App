üìò The Journey of Food Through Your Body

Digestion is the process that breaks down food into tiny nutrients your body can absorb and use. It starts in your mouth and ends in your intestines.

üó∫Ô∏è The Digestive System: Key Parts

  1. Mouth: Teeth chew food; saliva softens it and begins starch digestion.
  2. Stomach: Muscles churn food; acid and enzymes break proteins into smaller pieces.
  3. Small Intestine: Nutrients (sugars, amino acids, fatty acids) are absorbed into the blood.
  4. Large Intestine: Water is absorbed; waste becomes solid and is stored for removal.
