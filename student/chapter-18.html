üìò From Food to Fuel: The Final Stage of Digestion

After food is broken down in the stomach and small intestine, it becomes tiny molecules that your body can absorb and use for energy, growth, and repair.

ü©∏ How Absorption Happens

  • The **small intestine** has millions of tiny finger-like projections called **villi**.
  • Villi increase surface area‚Äîlike a sponge‚Äîto absorb nutrients quickly.
  • Glucose and amino acids enter the **bloodstream** directly.
  • Fatty acids enter the **lymphatic system**, then the blood.
  • Blood carries nutrients to every cell in your body!
