üìò What Is an Ecosystem?

An ecosystem is a community of living organisms (plants, animals, microbes) interacting with each other and their non-living environment (soil, water, sunlight, air). It is self-sustaining‚Äîmeaning it can maintain balance over time without outside help.

üå± Biotic vs. Abiotic Components

  • Biotic (living): Plants, animals, fungi, bacteria
  • Abiotic (non-living): Sunlight, water, soil, temperature, air

‚úÖ Example: In a pond ecosystem:
- Biotic: Fish, frogs, algae
- Abiotic: Water, sunlight, mud, oxygen
