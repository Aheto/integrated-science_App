üìò The Water Cycle: Nature‚Äôs Endless Loop

The **water cycle** is a natural process that moves water around Earth in a continuous, repeating pattern. It ensures that water is always available for plants, animals, and humans‚Äîeven though the total amount of water on Earth stays the same!

üåÄ The Four Key Stages

  1. Evaporation: Sun heats water in rivers, lakes, and oceans ‚Üí turns into water vapour (gas) ‚Üí rises into the air.
  2. Transpiration: Plants release water vapour from their leaves into the air.
  3. Condensation: Water vapour cools high in the atmosphere ‚Üí forms tiny droplets ‚Üí creates clouds.
  4. Precipitation: When cloud droplets become heavy, they fall as rain, hail, or snow.
