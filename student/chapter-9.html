üìò The Housefly: A Silent Disease Carrier

Houseflies may seem harmless, but they are dangerous **vectors**‚Äîorganisms that carry and spread disease-causing germs (**pathogens**) from waste to food without being sick themselves.

ü™∞ How Flies Feed and Spread Germs

  • Flies land on **manure, rotting food, dead animals, and open waste**.
  • They regurgitate digestive juices onto food to liquefy it‚Äîleaving behind bacteria and viruses.
  • Germs stick to their legs and bodies‚Äîand transfer to your food when they land on it.

‚ö†Ô∏è Three Major Harms to Humans

  1. Disease transmission: Dysentery, cholera, typhoid, and diarrhea.
  2. Food poisoning: Eating contaminated food causes vomiting, fever, and dehydration.
  3. Nuisance: Swarms of flies disturb meals, sleep, and concentration at school.
üí≠ Thought Experiment: A fly lands on uncovered soup, then on your hand. You eat with that hand. What diseases could you get? How could this be prevented?

üõ°Ô∏è Designing an Intervention

Use creativity and local materials to break the fly life cycle:

  • Cover food and water containers with lids or mesh.
  • Dispose of waste properly: Use covered bins; compost organic waste away from homes.
  • Make a fly trap: Use a plastic bottle, sugar water, and vinegar to attract and trap flies.
  • Keep surroundings clean: Sweep regularly; avoid stagnant water and open defecation.
